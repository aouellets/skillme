# CLOSER Sales Script

The intro consult is where a lead becomes a member. CLOSER is the sequence that
runs that conversation: Clarify, Label, Overview, Sell the vacation, Explain
concerns, Reinforce. It is a structured conversation, not a hard pitch. You earn
the sale by understanding the prospect, showing them the outcome, and making the
decision easy.

This skill is the consult itself. Objections get their own depth in
objection-handling-and-speed-to-lead, and the price you present comes from
gym-pricing-and-guarantees. The offer you are selling was built in
grand-slam-offer-builder.

## When to use this skill

Use it when the owner or a coach runs intro consults, wants to close more of
them, or asks for a sales script. Use it to prep before a consult and to review a
lost consult afterward.

## The CLOSER sequence as an executable script

Run the stages in order. Do not present price until you have done Clarify,
Label, Overview, and Sell. Price lands only after the prospect feels understood
and wants the outcome.

### C: Clarify why they came and what they want

Open warm, then get specific about the goal and the why behind it.

- "What made you book this today?"
- "What are you hoping to change in the next few months?"
- "Why now? What is different about this moment?"
Listen for the real driver: an event, a health scare, a feeling. Write down their
exact words; you will use them.

### L: Label them with the problem in their own words

Reflect the problem back so they feel understood and name it themselves.

- "So it sounds like the real issue is [their words], is that right?"
- "If I hear you, you have tried before but it never stuck because [their words]."
Get a yes. The label makes them the one who said the problem out loud.

### O: Overview their past pain and what they have tried

Walk through their history so they feel the cost of staying the same.

- "What have you tried so far?"
- "How did that go? What happened?"
- "What has that cost you, not just in money but in how you feel?"
This is not to shame them. It surfaces why the old way failed and sets up why a
coached, accountable approach is different.

### S: Sell the vacation, not the plane flight

Sell the outcome and the new life, not the features. They do not want sessions
and macros; they want to feel confident, keep up with their kids, fit the
clothes. Paint that, then connect the program to it.

- "Picture six weeks from now: [their dream outcome in their words]. That is what
  we build together."
- "Here is how we get you there: a coach who programs for you, a done-for-you
  plan so you never guess, a small group that keeps you showing up, and check-ins
  so we catch problems early."
Describe the destination first, then the vehicle. Keep features brief and always
tied to the outcome.

### E: Explain away concerns

Now, and only now, present the offer and the price, then handle whatever comes
up. See references/price-presentation for how to present and then stay silent.

- Present: "The 6-week challenge is [price], and it includes [the stack]. If you
  do the work and do not get [the result], we keep coaching you free."
- Then stop talking. Let them respond.
- For price, time, spouse, or "let me think about it," run the response framework
  in objection-handling-and-speed-to-lead: acknowledge, reframe, isolate, close.

### R: Reinforce the decision after they buy

The moment they say yes, confirm they made a great choice and remove buyer's
remorse.

- "You made the right call. People who start with a coach get there far faster
  than going it alone."
- Lock the next step immediately: first session booked, plan sent, group access
  given, payment confirmed. Momentum prevents cancellations.

## Transitions that hold the structure

Use these to move between stages without it feeling like a script:

- Clarify to Label: "Let me make sure I have this right."
- Overview to Sell: "Here is the good news. This is fixable, and here is how."
- Sell to price: "Let me show you exactly what that looks like and what it
  costs."
- After yes: "Perfect. Let us get you started right now."

```
  -> [collect payment, book session 1, grant group access]
```

## references/closer-framework

Each stage, its purpose, and the common mistake:

- Clarify. Purpose: surface the real goal and the emotional driver. Mistake:
  rushing to pitch before you know why they came.
- Label. Purpose: make the prospect name the problem so they own it. Mistake:
  telling them their problem instead of reflecting theirs.
- Overview. Purpose: let them feel the cost of the status quo. Mistake: shaming,
  or skipping it so there is no urgency.
- Sell the vacation. Purpose: attach the program to the life they want. Mistake:
  listing features and prices before desire exists.
- Explain concerns. Purpose: present price and dissolve objections. Mistake:
  talking past the price, discounting at the first hesitation, or not staying
  silent after the number.
- Reinforce. Purpose: cement the decision and start momentum. Mistake: ending at
  yes without booking the first session, which invites cancellation.

The order is the method. Price before desire kills the sale; desire before price
makes it easy.

## references/price-presentation

How to present the price:

- Anchor first. State the stacked value, then the price, so the number lands
  against the value, not the wallet.
- Say it plainly and with confidence. "It's 499." No hedging, no apology, no
  rushing to justify. Hesitation tells the prospect the price is negotiable or
  unfair.
- Then stay silent. After the number, the next person to speak loses ground. Let
  the prospect process and respond. Silence is not awkward; it is respect for
  their decision.
- Offer a payment plan before you offer a discount. A plan preserves the price
  and the value; a discount erodes both. If they cannot do the full amount, split
  it, do not cut it.

When an objection comes, hand to objection-handling-and-speed-to-lead and run
acknowledge, reframe, isolate, close.
