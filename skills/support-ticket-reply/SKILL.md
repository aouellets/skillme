# Support Ticket Reply

Every support reply has one job: give the customer exactly what they need to move forward, without making them write back. Lead with the resolution, follow with context, close with a clear next step.

## Reply structure (always in this order)

1. Acknowledge the specific issue in one sentence — name it, do not paraphrase vaguely.
2. State the resolution or the concrete next action immediately.
3. Explain the 'why' only if it reduces future confusion or builds trust. Keep it to two sentences.
4. Close with a single, unambiguous next step: a link, a yes/no question, or a confirmation request.
5. Sign off warmly but briefly.

## Tone calibration

Match urgency level to ticket severity. Use plain language — no jargon, no product-internal terms. For frustrated customers, name the frustration before moving to the fix: 'That should not have happened, and I am sorry it did.' Never use hollow phrases like 'Great question!' or 'I totally understand your frustration' without immediately proving it.

## One-touch discipline

Before sending, run a mental check: does this reply answer every question in the ticket? If the customer asks three things, address all three, numbered. Partial answers guarantee a follow-up and damage CSAT. If a full answer requires information you do not have, say what you are doing to get it and give a time window.

## What to avoid

- Do not open with 'I' — start with the customer or the action.
- Do not paste policy text verbatim; translate it into what it means for this customer.
- Do not hedge with 'should' or 'might' when you know the answer; use 'will' or 'is'.
- Do not apologize more than once; repeated apologies shift focus from resolution to performance.

## Templates

Issue confirmed and resolved: 'Your [specific issue] is now fixed — [what changed]. You will see [outcome] within [timeframe]. Let me know if anything looks off.'

Issue requires investigation: 'I have flagged [specific issue] to our [team] and will follow up by [day/time] with a status update. In the meantime, [workaround if any].'

Feature request acknowledged: 'I have logged this as a feature request under [theme]. I cannot promise a timeline, but your use case is now on record. I will reach out if it ships.'
